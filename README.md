# International Students in the United Kingdom - Barriers & Pathways
## By Fawaz Ayofe | University of Sunderland

## My Story
Leaving home to study in the United Kingdom felt like stepping into a new chapter, one filled with ambition, uncertainty, and hope.
Like many international students, I wasn’t just pursuing a degree; I was investing in a future, often with significant financial and personal sacrifice.
At the University of Sunderland, I came to understand how fragile that journey can sometimes be.
After failing to meet the payment deadline, I temporarily lost access to essential academic tools like Canvas, Teams for virtual classes and my student portal. 

In an instant, lectures, assignments, and deadlines became out of reach, not because I lacked commitment, but because of a moment of financial strain.
I remember the anxiety of watching time pass while being locked out of my own education, and the difficult decision to take out a loan just to regain access and keep going.
It was a moment that many international students quietly face, balancing determination with pressure that often goes unseen.
This project grew from that experience. It tells a broader story, not just mine, but of thousands of students navigating similar challenges.
By combining data with lived experience, it aims to shed light on these moments and explore how universities can better support the people behind the numbers.

## What This Project Analyses
  - Where international students come from and which countries are growing fastest
  - How many students meet tuition fee payment deadlines across 11 UK universities
  - How flexible each university's installment plan is
  - How many graduates secure sponsored jobs after graduation
  - Recommendations for universities to better support international students

## Key Findings
  - India is the fastest-growing source of international students, up 17,705 in one year
  - Sunderland has the highest default rate (20%) despite offering the most flexible installment plans
  - Only 1 in 3 Graduate Route visa holders secure a sponsored job
  - Blocking Canvas access for missed payments increases student stress without improving payment
  rates

## Recommendations
  - Never block Canvas or the student portal for missed payments
  - Extend the installment period until graduation
  - Partner with employers before students graduate
  - Assign a financial advisor to every international student

## Data Sources
  - HESA (Higher Education Statistics Agency) - international student numbers
  - Home Office Immigration Statistics - Graduate Route visa data
  - NUS International Student Finance Survey - payment outcome estimates
  - University websites - installment plan details
